Undergraduate Research Intern @ Illinois Security and Privacy Lab

September 2023 - Present

As an undergraduate researcher at the Illinois Security and Privacy Lab, I collaborate with graduate students to analyze the privacy of end-to-end encrypted messaging platforms. I set up a wireless hotspot using a mini-computer and Ubuntu, connected two phones, and collected data from apps like Element, Signal, and WhatsApp. Using Wireshark and MITM Proxy, I analyzed packet lengths, HTTP protocols, and message timing to assess the feasibility of third-party tracking between communicating devices. This project strengthened my expertise in network analysis and privacy research.

Threat Intelligence Intern @ New Jersey Cybersecurity and Communications Integration Cell

June 2023 - August 2023

As an Information Technology Intern at NJCCIC, I analyzed key threat actors and security attacks targeting New Jersey’s public and private sectors. I conducted demos on SQL injections, man-in-the-middle, and DDoS attacks, providing detailed prevention documentation. For my final project, I assessed a threat actor impacting small businesses by stealing financial and confidential data and presented my findings to officials from NJ Homeland Security and the U.S. Department of State, enhancing my expertise in cybersecurity and threat analysis.

Recent Projects

Quantum Money Simulator

Under the guidance of Professor Shabani at New York University, I explored the concept of Quantum Money, a secure, forgery-proof currency system based on quantum mechanics.

Impact: Designed and implemented a quantum money simulator using Qiskit, enabling users to securely transfer quantum notes and prevent third-party interference in private-sector quantum schemes.

Technologies: Python, Qiskit, IBM Quantum Simulation, Quantum Key Distribution, Quantum Circuit Simulation

Key Features:

  • Implemented private and public-key quantum money schemes inspired by Stephen Wiesner’s foundational model
  • Demonstrated the no-cloning theorem to prevent counterfeiting
  • Simulated resilience to noise and error impacts in quantum systems to enhance security
  • Facilitated secure transactions showcasing potential applications for government and commercial sectors
