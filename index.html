Software Engineer @ Gies Disruption Lab

Feb 2024 - December 2024

I worked on developing solutions for client-oriented businesses. My first client was Jinship, a startup focused on creating an AI powered service to help people in underrepresented companies find scholarships related to their interests. As a backend software engineer, I developed a Large Language Model using Llama and Mistral that searches and tags all the scholarship funds that are available and groups them by category. I am working on creating a backend database that connects to the company’s energy model using VectorAI and Google Cloud Platform.

Undergraduate Research Intern @ Illinois Security and Privacy Lab

September 2023 - Present

As an undergraduate researcher for the Illinois Security and Privacy Lab, I am working on a project with masters and PhD students to track the privacy of sending messages over end-to-end encrypted platforms. For the first step of this project, I created a wireless hotspot using a mini-computer and connected it to an Ubuntu platform using Ethernet. Now, I am working on collecting data by connecting two phones to this hotspot, and tracking their messages using apps, such as Element, Signal, and WhatApp. Using Wireshark and setting up a web proxy using MITM Proxy, I analyzed the packet length data, HTTP protocols, and the timing differences between each message to determine whether it is possible for a 3rd party source to track the two phones that are communicating with each other.

Threat Intelligence Intern @ New Jersey Cybersecurity and Communications Integration Cell

June 2023 - August 2023

As an Information Technology Intern at the NJCCIC, I learned and analyzed key threat actors and common security attacks that target both private and public sectors in the state of New Jersey. I worked closely with professionals from state schooling departments and learned about networking practices administered to students and teachers. I also conducted demos of how SQL injections, man-in-the-middle attacks, and DDoS attacks happen, and provided walkthrough and a complete documentation of how to prevent such attacks. For my final project, I worked with small businesses, and analyzed how a prevalent threat actor is harming these businesses by stealing vital financial and confidential information. I presented my report to officials from the NJ Homeland Security Office and U.S. Department of State officials.

Recent Projects

Quantum Money Simulator

Under the guidance of Professor Shabani at New York University, I explored the concept of Quantum Money, a secure, forgery-proof currency system based on quantum mechanics.

Impact: Designed and implemented a quantum money simulator using Qiskit, enabling users to securely transfer quantum notes and prevent third-party interference in private-sector quantum schemes.

Technologies: Python, Qiskit, IBM Quantum Simulation, Quantum Key Distribution, Quantum Circuit Simulation

Key Features:

  • Implemented private and public-key quantum money schemes inspired by Stephen Wiesner’s foundational model
  • Demonstrated the no-cloning theorem to prevent counterfeiting
  • Simulated resilience to noise and error impacts in quantum systems to enhance security
  • Facilitated secure transactions showcasing potential applications for government and commercial sectors
